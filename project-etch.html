Automation of Electro-Etch Marking Process

3-DOF robotic arm + trajectory planning + stable dwell/pressure for repeatable stencil marking.

What

  • Automated a manual stencil-based electro-etch marking step using a compact 3-DOF robotic arm.
  • Objective: stabilize dwell time and contact force to reduce non-conformance and operator dependency.
  • Delivered a prototype suitable for integration around existing workstations.

How

  • Modeled the arm in CATIA; planned coverage paths and joint profiles in Robo-Analyser.
  • Implemented closed-loop routines for dwell timing and normal force consistency at the stencil.
  • Integrated a simple electronics stack with Bluetooth control for operator-initiated cycles.
  • Prepared wiring/controls documentation for quick shop-floor deployment and maintenance.

Results

  • Stabilized dwell/pressure and improved end-effector placement repeatability.
  • Large reduction in non-conformance compared with the manual process.
  • Compact, low-cost prototype ready for incremental scale-up at the line.
