6-Jaw Chuck for Anti-Polishing Ring

Uniform clamping for thin-walled rings — consolidate operations, preserve circularity, improve yield.

What

  • Designed a six-jaw workholding concept for thin-walled anti-polishing rings with internal slots.
  • Goal: maintain tight circularity while reducing re-clamps and setup-induced distortion.
  • Targeted a robust, manufacturable chuck layout with clear assembly documentation.

How

  • Synchronized inner and outer jaws to distribute clamping forces uniformly across the thin section.
  • Defined operation flow to complete more than six machining steps in a single clamp.
  • Prepared exploded views and 2D drawings to communicate manufacturing and assembly.

Results

  • Maintained approximately 20 µm circularity on thin-walled rings during machining.
  • Consolidated >6 operations into a single setup, reducing cycle time and handling error.
  • Lowered overall cost and improved repeatability through better force distribution.
